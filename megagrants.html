About Me

Hi, my name is Henry Ventura.

I have a software development background of working on full-stack web products since 2013. Over the course of my career, I worked at a tech incubator, moved to the Bay Area to participate in startup-life, and led front-end development on a new Google Cloud product.

I began to dive into motion graphics In 2018 and instantly fell in love with how it felt to create through these kinds of tools.

I released my first video short in early 2020 as I began to step away from web-work and dip into my savings to explore this new world. While I am very grateful for the opportunities that Unity has provided me in the past, Unreal Engine's built-in solutions for virtual cinematography is what brought me to the other side of the table.

Empowered with the creative tools in Unreal Engine and fueled by my love of mechs, it became clear what my next objective in life became. I quickly put out a look-dev trailer of Royal Division and received overwelmingly positive feedback.

Unreal Engine has been an absolute privilege and joy to work with. It enables me to do fun weekend projects without slowing down the rest of development.

This past year and a half of working on Royal Division have been the most fun days of my life. Being able to create experiences in an interactive medium is truly a privilege, and I am excited to have folks hop in the cockpit to start creating memories of their own.

I think it is important to mention that, in context to creating a game with mechs, that this isn't a "mech game". It is a game to deliver feelings, interactions, and shared experiences, and mechs are just a component of the package.

Next Steps

  • Complete art assets for the Mech

    At present, the art assets for the mech are being commissioned out on a shoe-string budget. As such, commissions are being done iteratively at a trepditious rate, in a bid to uncover unknowns and reduce early sunk cost but at the expense of time.

    Ideally, we would use the grant to hire a 3D generalist to spearhead these tasks.

    If the grant was not large enough to pay for a salary, it could still help us by being able to afford more substantial commissions so that development may progress at a faster pace.

  • Create bespoke terrain

    Terrain has a special place in my heart. It is the mirror that which gameplay systems reflect off of. It is the canvas that allows the player to paint their own stories onto.

    Thanks to the Unreal Marketplace (product), we were able to figure out at minimal-cost the type of terrain topology that complements gameplay.

    Given the opportunity, the next priority after the mech is to create a bespoke terrain that is able to leverage existing gameplay systems to maximum effect.

    Similar to the mech, ideally we would use the grant to hire a 3D generalist to help with this task. Otherwise, a smaller amount of grant money can help with affording commisions.
